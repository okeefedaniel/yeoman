Yeoman User Manual

Comprehensive guide to speaking-engagement and event-scheduling workflows in the DockLabs suite.

FAQ

Overview

Yeoman is the speaking-engagement and event-scheduling product in the DockLabs suite. It moves an invitation — for a hearing, keynote, panel, ribbon-cutting, site visit, or any other ask on the principal's time — through public intake, triage, decision, delegation, calendar push, and completion, with a full audit trail.

The lifecycle: public intake → triage → decision (accept / tentative / decline) → optional delegation → calendar push → completion. Yeoman doesn't store bills, FOIA requests, or grants — those live in the products that own them.

Public intake

A self-serve form anyone can submit, with a status URL the submitter can revisit.

Workflow engine

Every transition role-gated and audited via Keel's WorkflowEngine.

Calendar & iCal

Push to Outlook / Exchange / Google. Per-invitation .ics download. Email a calendar invite to anyone.

Map & distance

Auto-geocoded venues, geographic plot, and driving distance from named reference addresses.

Roles

Yeoman roles are issued via Keel SSO and surfaced in your sidebar profile.

RoleCapability
Administrator Full access. Triage, decide, delegate, schedule, complete, cancel, reopen. Manages the principal profile and reference addresses.
Scheduler Operates the queue. Starts review, requests info, pushes to calendar, completes, and cancels. Cannot accept / decline / delegate.
Delegate Reads invitations delegated to them. Receives delegation notifications.
Viewer Read-only across the dashboard, list, and calendar.

Suite-level system_admin inherits Administrator behavior on Yeoman.

Getting Started

Signing in
1 From any DockLabs product, click Yeoman in the fleet switcher, or visit https://yeoman.docklabs.ai/.
2 Click Sign in with DockLabs (the suite OIDC button).
3 You'll land on /dashboard/.
Yeoman keeps allauth mounted at /auth/ (preserving the registered OIDC redirect URI), while the canonical login form lives at /accounts/login/ like the rest of the suite. The legacy /auth/login/ 301-redirects to the canonical path.
What you'll see first
  • Dashboard — counts by status, your queue, today's events, and upcoming events.
  • Invitations — full list with filters.
  • Calendar — month/week/day view of every event.
  • Map — geographic plot of in-person events.
  • Reports — activity rollups and CSV export.

Dashboard

/dashboard/ — the staff landing page. Surfaces:

  • Status counters — quick view of how many invitations sit in Received, Under Review, Needs Info, Accepted, Tentative, Scheduled, Delegated, Declined, Completed, Cancelled.
  • My queue — invitations where you are the assignee, the principal, or the delegate, ordered by event date.
  • Today — events happening today.
  • Upcoming — the next 7–30 days.

The dashboard is read-oriented; click any invitation to act on it.

Invitations

/invitations/ — the operational list of every invitation in your agency, with filters for status, priority, format, modality, date range, assignee, and tag.

Anatomy of an invitation

Each invitation carries:

  • Submitter info — first/last name, email, phone, organization, title.
  • Event details — name, description, date, start/end time, timezone, format, modality.
  • Location — venue name, full address, lat/lng (auto-geocoded), or virtual platform + link.
  • Assignment — assigned coordinator, principal, and optional delegate.
  • Priority — Low, Normal, High, Urgent.
  • Tags — agency-defined labels (e.g. legislative, economic-dev, education).
  • Logistics — expected attendees, surrogate-OK flag, press expected, will-be-recorded.
  • Outcome — decline reason, calendar event ID, push timestamp, calendar invite recipients.
  • Status history — every transition logged with actor and comment.
  • Attachments — uploaded files (briefing materials, agendas, related decks).
  • Notes — internal staff notes.
  • Beacon status — has the submitter been pushed to Beacon as a contact?
  • Status token — UUID powering the public submitter status page.

Format options

Presentation, Keynote, Panel Moderator, Panel Participant, Site Visit, Roundtable, Ribbon Cutting, Meeting, Reception, Conference, Fireside Chat, Tour, Other.

Assignment, Principal & Delegation

Yeoman tracks three independent user pointers on every invitation:

PointerWho it points to
assigned_toCoordinator — the staff member managing logistics and submitter communication. Set via Claim; cleared via Unclaim.
principalSpeaker — the Commissioner or agency head whose time is being scheduled.
delegated_toStand-in — when the principal can't attend, an Administrator delegates to a deputy.

Claim / Unclaim

Open an invitation → click Claim to take ownership. You become assigned_to. Click Unclaim to release; the invitation returns to the pool.

Delegate

From an Accepted or Tentative invitation, an Administrator picks a delegate, optionally adds delegation notes, and the invitation moves to the Delegated status. The delegate receives an invitation_delegated notification. The full chain is preserved in DelegationLog on the invitation detail page.

Notes & Attachments

Notes

Internal staff notes live alongside the invitation (InvitationNote). Notes are staff-only — submitters never see them. Use them for internal reasoning, conflicts to flag, or coordination context.

Attachments

Drag-and-drop file uploads on the invitation detail page. Each attachment carries an original filename, content type, size, the user who uploaded it, a flag distinguishing staff-uploaded from submitter-uploaded files, and an optional label.

Files are scanned by keel.security.FileSecurityValidator on upload to enforce size and extension policy.

Calendar & iCal

/calendar/ — month/week/day FullCalendar view of every invitation with an event_date, color-coded by status. Click any event to open the invitation detail page.

Per-invitation iCal export

/invitations/<uuid>/ical/ — download a single .ics file for an invitation. Useful when you want to forward an event to someone outside the suite without granting them Yeoman access.

Send calendar invite

/invitations/<uuid>/send-calendar/ — email a calendar invite (via keel.calendar) to one or more recipients. The recipients are stored on the invitation in calendar_sent_to along with the time and the sender, so future audits can see who got it.

Push to external calendar

Invitations in Accepted, Tentative, or Delegated status can be pushed to Outlook / Exchange via keel.calendar.push_event. The external event ID is stored as calendar_event_id and the timestamp as calendar_pushed_at. Cancelling a Scheduled invitation calls back to cancel_event so the external event is removed.

Map

/map/ — geographic plot of in-person and hybrid invitations. Each marker is one invitation, colored by status. Use it to spot scheduling clusters, evaluate driving distance from the principal's reference addresses, and plan travel days.

Geocoding runs automatically on save when a venue address is present and coordinates are not yet recorded. If geocoding fails, the invitation is still created — it just doesn't appear on the map until staff fix the address.

Public Intake

/invite/ — the public-facing form anyone can use to request the principal's time. Captures submitter identity, event details, location/virtual fields, attachments, and contextual flags (press expected, will-be-recorded, surrogate-OK).

What happens on submission

1Yeoman creates an Invitation row in the received status.
2Geocodes the venue address if present.
3Fires an invitation_received notification to schedulers and administrators in the agency.
4Issues a status_token UUID to the submitter so they can return to /invite/status/<token>/ and check progress without logging in.
5(If configured) Pushes the submitter to Beacon as a contact.

Programmatic intake

/api/v1/intake/invitation/ — bearer-token auth endpoint for forwarding from another website or a legacy system.

Submitter status page

/invite/status/<status_token>/ — anyone holding the token URL can see the current status, the assigned coordinator's name (when available), and the event date once confirmed. They cannot see internal notes, attachments other than ones they uploaded themselves, or any information about other invitations.

Reports & Exports

/reports/ — activity dashboard:

  • Invitation volume by month / quarter.
  • Acceptance rate.
  • Median time-to-decision and time-to-schedule.
  • Breakdowns by format, modality, and tag.
  • Top organizations and top regions.

CSV export

/reports/export/ — download a CSV of every invitation (filtered by the current report parameters) with status, dates, assignee, principal, delegate, format, modality, and outcome fields. Suitable for finance or annual-report rollups.

Beacon CRM Sync

When the suite is deployed with Beacon, Yeoman can push every submitter to Beacon as a contact, capturing the speaking-engagement relationship.

Add to Beacon

On an invitation detail page, an Administrator sees an Add to Beacon control if BEACON_INTAKE_URL and BEACON_INTAKE_API_KEY are configured. Click it, and Yeoman:

  • Posts the submitter to Beacon's intake API.
  • Records the resulting Beacon contact ID on the invitation (beacon_contact_id).
  • Sets beacon_status='added' and stamps the time + actor.
  • Drops a provenance entry on the Beacon contact pointing back to this Yeoman invitation.
If Beacon isn't configured, the button is hidden — there are no silent no-ops. The control reappears once Beacon is reachable.

Helm Inbox

Yeoman exposes /api/v1/helm-feed/inbox/ — Helm's per-user companion endpoint. When you open Helm's Today tab, the Awaiting Me column queries this endpoint and lists Yeoman invitations where you are the gating dependency:

  • Coordinate — open invitations assigned to you.
  • Speak — open invitations where you are the principal.
  • Cover for — open invitations delegated to you.

The event_date drives the due_date. The link returns you to the Yeoman invitation detail page in one hop.

EventWhen it fires

invitation_assignedAn invitation was claimed or assigned to you.
invitation_delegatedAn invitation was delegated to you.
invitation_acceptedAn invitation was accepted (email channel by default).

invitation_status_changedThe status of an invitation you're on changed (in-app channel by default).

Channels (in-app + email) are user-configurable at /notifications/preferences/. The link is in the sidebar user-menu dropdown.

Principal Settings

/settings/principal/ — Administrator-only configuration of the principal whose time Yeoman is scheduling.

  • Display name — e.g. "Commissioner O'Keefe".
  • Title — e.g. "Commissioner of Economic Development".
  • Email + phone — internal contact info.
  • Notes — anything reviewers should know (preferred briefing format, dietary needs, etc.).
  • Reference addresses — named locations (Home, Office, Capitol) used to compute driving distance from incoming invitations. One can be marked the default; sort order controls how they appear on each invitation.

There is one principal profile per agency. Multi-principal deployments use multiple agencies.

Status Reference

Invitation status flow

Received Under Review Needs Info Accepted / Tentative Delegated Scheduled Completed

Plus terminal exits: Declined (comment required) and Cancelled. Both are reopenable to Under Review by an Administrator.

Priority
